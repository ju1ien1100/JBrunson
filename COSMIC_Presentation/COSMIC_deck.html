A soundtrack for every panel, generated automatically

Drop in a comic page. C.O.S.M.I.C detects the panels, understands each one, and composes a custom three-layer soundtrack — then plays the page back panel-by-panel, like a motion comic.

C.O.S.M.I.CArchitecture
Component · 1

Panel detection that doesn't give up

The panel is the unit of pacing — so finding it reliably across wildly different art styles is the foundation of the whole product.

Primary · fast & free

👁️ kumiko (computer vision)

Classic CV gutter/border detection. Instant and zero-cost. Trusted whenever it confidently finds 2+ panels.

Fallback · robust

🧠 Claude grid detection

When borders vanish — borderless / dark / splash pages — Claude (Sonnet 4.6) maps each panel onto a 10×10 grid and returns ordered boxes.

🧭 Reading-order aware

Detection also infers reading direction — manga right-to-left, western left-to-right, webtoon top-to-bottom — so panels and dialogue come out in the order a human would actually read them. Always degrades gracefully to a whole-page panel.

C.O.S.M.I.CPanel Detection
Component · 2

One look → a complete audio brief

Each cropped panel goes to Claude vision once and comes back as a structured brief that drives all three audio models.

🌊 Music prompt

A rich background-track description — genre, instrumentation, mood — for Stable Audio.

🎭 Mood

One of 7 moods — calm · tense · action · sad · mysterious · triumphant · neutral — picks the melody.

💬 Dialogue

Speech-bubble text transcribed verbatim, in reading order, original language preserved.

🗣️ Voice

Chooses a male or female voice that matches the panel's dominant speaker.

Structured output, not guesswork. Claude returns a typed schema (Pydantic-validated), so every field is safe to feed straight into the generation step — no fragile parsing.

C.O.S.M.I.CAudio Layers
Deep dive

The mood → music engine

A hand-crafted motif library is the bridge between "this panel feels tense" and actual notes a model can perform.

  • 7 curated motifs — each mood is a sequence of (pitch, hold, gap) notes (e.g. action = a fast driving riff; sad = a slow minor descent).
  • Frame-accurate — 1 frame = 40 ms, mapping 1:1 to Magenta's note conditioning and to MIDI ticks.
  • Exports real .mid files — pure-Python, zero dependencies, openable in any DAW (Ableton-ready).

Mood palette

😌 calm😰 tense 💥 action😢 sad 🌫️ mysterious🎺 triumphant ➖ neutral

The motif conditions Magenta RT2, which performs it in the style from the scene's music prompt — structure from us, timbre from the model.

C.O.S.M.I.CTech Stack
Why it's hard

Problems we actually solved

🧭 Reading order

Manga's right-to-left flow breaks naïve OCR. We infer direction per page so dialogue is voiced in the right sequence.

🔲 Borderless panels

Splash & dark pages have no gutters for CV. The Claude grid fallback recovers panels by narrative grouping.

🗣️ Voice ≠ music

Early dialogue came out sung. A dedicated "spoken-word, dry vocal" style + preset voices yields clear speech.

⏳ Latency

Three models would mean a long wait. Warm GPUs + progressive unlock make the first panel feel immediate.
